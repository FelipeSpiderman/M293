Things to Do in Summer

Fun DIY Projects for Sunny Days

By Jordan Kim

Long summer days are the perfect canvas for creativity. Getting hands-on with a do-it-yourself (DIY) project is a fantastic way to unplug, learn a new skill, and create something unique and personal. These simple and fun projects are perfect for a sunny afternoon and require minimal supplies.

1. Painted Garden Stones

Transform ordinary stones from your yard into colorful works of art. This is a wonderfully relaxing activity for all ages.

  • What you'll need: Smooth, flat stones, acrylic paints, small paintbrushes, and a clear sealant (like Mod Podge Outdoor).
  • How to do it: Wash and dry the stones thoroughly. Paint your designs—ladybugs, mandalas, encouraging words, or abstract patterns. Let them dry completely, then apply a coat or two of sealant to protect your art from the elements. They make great decorations for your garden or thoughtful gifts.

2. DIY Citronella Candles

Keep pesky mosquitos at bay with your own homemade, beautifully scented candles. It's easier than you think and fills your outdoor space with a pleasant aroma.

  • What you'll need: Soy wax flakes, candle wicks, heat-proof containers (like mason jars), and citronella essential oil. You can also add other oils like lavender or lemongrass.
  • How to do it: Melt the wax flakes in a double boiler or a pot over low heat. While it melts, secure the wicks to the bottom of your containers. Once the wax is liquid, let it cool slightly before stirring in the essential oils. Carefully pour the wax into the containers and let them set for several hours.

3. Pressed Flower Art

Capture the beauty of summer blooms by pressing them to create delicate, timeless art.

  • What you'll need: Freshly picked flowers, a heavy book, and parchment paper.
  • How to do it: Place the flowers between two sheets of parchment paper and press them inside a heavy book. Leave them for 2-4 weeks. Once they are completely flat and dry, you can carefully arrange them in a picture frame, use them to decorate cards, or even embed them in clear resin coasters.

These projects are not just about the final product, but about the joy of the creative process itself—a perfect way to make your summer even more memorable.
