Things to Do in Summer

Summer Reading List: Must-Read Books

By Taylor Brooks

There's a special magic to summer reading. The longer days and relaxed pace provide the perfect opportunity to escape into a different world, learn something new, or simply get lost in a great story. Whether you're at the beach, in a park, or on your own back porch, a good book is the perfect summer companion.

Building the Perfect, Balanced List

A great summer reading list, like a great summer playlist, has variety. Instead of sticking to one genre, try building a list that covers different moods and interests. This ensures you always have the right book for the right moment. Consider including:

  • The Page-Turner: A fast-paced thriller or mystery that you can't put down. Perfect for a long travel day.
  • The Thought-Provoker: A non-fiction book that explores a fascinating topic, teaches you a new skill, or changes your perspective.
  • The Heart-Warmer: A light, character-driven novel or romance that feels like a warm hug. Ideal for a lazy afternoon.
  • The Classic You've Always Meant to Read: Summer is the perfect time to finally tackle that famous book you've heard so much about.

Our Top Picks for This Summer

To get you started, here are a few recommendations that fit the bill:

For a Thrill: "The Silent Patient" by Alex Michaelides
A gripping psychological thriller with a twist so shocking, you'll want to immediately re-read it. It's the definition of a page-turner.

For a New Perspective: "Atomic Habits" by James Clear
This isn't your typical self-help book. Clear provides an incredibly practical and actionable framework for building good habits and breaking bad ones, one tiny change at a time.

For a Feel-Good Escape: "The House in the Cerulean Sea" by T.J. Klune
A wonderfully charming and heartwarming fantasy novel about a caseworker sent to investigate an orphanage of magical children. It's a story about kindness and finding your chosen family.
