Things to Do in Summer

Top 5 Outdoor Adventures for Summer

By Riley Evans

Summer is a season of energy, long days, and warm sunshine, practically begging us to step outside and explore. While relaxing by the pool has its merits, embarking on an adventure can create lasting memories and recharge your spirit. Here are five accessible outdoor adventures to make the most of your summer.

1. Explore a Local Hiking Trail

You don't need to travel to a national park to find a great hike. Almost every area has local parks, nature preserves, or state forests with marked trails. Use apps like AllTrails to find a route that matches your fitness level. Hiking offers a perfect blend of exercise and nature immersion, allowing you to discover hidden streams, scenic viewpoints, and local wildlife.

2. Go Kayaking or Paddleboarding

Seeing the world from the water offers a completely new perspective. Renting a kayak or a stand-up paddleboard (SUP) for an afternoon is a fantastic way to cool off and get a great workout. It's peaceful, meditative, and allows you to explore coastlines, lakeshores, or riverbanks that are inaccessible on foot.

3. Have a Stargazing Picnic

This adventure begins after the sun goes down. Pack some snacks, a blanket, and drive just a short distance away from city lights. Lay back and watch the night sky come alive. Use a stargazing app to identify constellations, planets, and maybe even catch a shooting star. It's a humbling and awe-inspiring experience.

4. Visit a "You-Pick" Farm

Combine a fun outing with a delicious reward. In the summer, many local farms open their fields for visitors to pick their own berries, peaches, or other seasonal produce. It's a great activity for families and friends, connecting you directly to where your food comes from.

5. Try Forest Bathing (Shinrin-yoku)

This isn't about taking a bath in the woods. "Shinrin-yoku" is the Japanese practice of simply being in nature and connecting with it through your senses. Find a quiet spot in a forest, turn off your phone, and just *be*. Listen to the birds, feel the breeze, smell the earth, and observe the light filtering through the leaves. It's a proven way to lower stress and boost your mood.
